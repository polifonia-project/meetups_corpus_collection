Herman Junior Cook (July 22, 1934 – February 3, 1992) was an American hard bop tenor saxophone player.


== Biography ==
Cook was born in Pensacola, Florida, United States. After playing with Dizzy Gillespie in 1958, Cook was a member of the Horace Silver Quintet (1958–1964); when Silver left the group in the hands of Blue Mitchell Cook stayed in the quintet for five more years (1964–1969). Later associations included Freddie Hubbard, Elvin Jones, George Coleman, Louis Hayes (1975–1976), Bill Hardman (1979–1989), and the McCoy Tyner big band.In addition to many appearances as a sideman, Junior Cook recorded as a leader for Jazzland (1961), Catalyst (1977), Muse, and SteepleChase.
He also taught at Berklee School of Music for a year during the 1970s.In the early 1990s, Cook was playing with Clifford Jordan, and also leading his own group. He died in February 1992, in his apartment in New York City, aged 57.
