Nikolai Semyonovich Rabinovich (Russian: Николай Семенович Рабинович) (7 October 1908- 26 July 1972) was a Russian conductor and teacher.

Rabinovich studied under Nikolai Malko and graduated from the Leningrad Conservatory in 1931, becoming a professor in 1968. He trained a series of notable conductors including Yuri Simonov, Neeme Järvi, Vladislav Chachin, Vitaliy Kutsenko, and Victor Yampolsky.He was director of the Mikhaylovsky Theatre 1944–1948.
