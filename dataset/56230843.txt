Jo Woo-chan (Korean: 조우찬; born January 20, 2005), is a South Korean rapper. He was a contestant on Show Me the Money 6. He released his first collaborative single, "OGZ", on January 5, 2018.


== Early life ==
Woo-chan was born on January 20, 2005 in Seoul, South Korea. Previously Woo-chan already had some experience of working as a child actor and model, filming commercials for a variety of brands.


== Career ==


=== 2017–2019: Show Me the Money 6, OG School Project, departure from Cube ===
At just 12 years old, Jo Woo-chan was the youngest participant in the history of Show Me the Money 6 and dubbed "Lil Dok2" in 2017. During the show, he released a few collaboration singles like "1/N" with Team Dynamic Duo and "What You Call Is The Price" featuring Nucksal, as well as a solo track "VVIP" featuring Sik-K. Jo was eliminated in the semi-finals. As a guest, Jo featured on Hui's song "Wake me up" in KBS's pilot music variety show, Hyena on the Keyboard; the single was released on October 9 on Korean online music sites. In October 2017, Jo was cast in the JTBC short film Father’s Sword by Director Jeong Yoon-cheol.On January 5, 2018, Jo took part in a joint project group called OG School Project with Starship trainees Park Hyun-jin and Achillo, they released the song "OGZ", produced by GroovyRoom.In July 2019, Jo made his departure from Cube Entertainment.


=== 2022–present: Trainee A ===
On January 8, 2022, Jo was revealed as a member of Big Hit Music's upcoming boy group with the tentative name Trainee A.
