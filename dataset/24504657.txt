Braeden Matthew Lemasters (born January 27, 1996) is an American actor, musician, and voice actor. He is best known as a founding member of the band Wallows and for his role as Albert in Men of a Certain Age.


== Life and career ==
Braeden was born in Warren, Ohio, to Dave and Michelle Lemasters. In 2005, He began his acting career at the age of 9, playing the role of Frankie in the popular television series Six Feet Under. In 2006, he played the role of a child with Autism syndrome in the famous US television series House Subsequently, he starred in Criminal Minds , ER, The Closer and finally worked as a voice actor in the video game Thrillville.In 2007, he played the role of Jacob Marshall-LaHaye in the film Love's Unending Legacy. He later starred in Sacrifices of the Heart, Life, Grey's Anatomy and Wainy Days. 
From 2009, Lemasters starred as Albert Tranelli in the series Men of a Certain Age for which he was nominated best performance in a TV series in the 32nd Young Artist Awards. He was in the show for two seasons until it ended in 2011.
He also starred in The Stepfather as Sean Harding in 2009, the film was directed by Nelson McCormick. It received negative reviews from critics and grossed over $31 million.In 2010 he appeared in the film Easy A, as young version of Penn Badgley's character Todd. He starred as Ralphie Parker in the sequel film of A Christmas Story in 2012.From 2013-2014, Lemasters starred as Victor McAllister in the ABC original series Betrayal In 2015, Lemasters starred in the horror comedy film R.L. Stine's Monsterville: Cabinet of Souls which was directed by Peter DeLuise. The film was based on the novel by R.L. Stine of the same name.


=== Wallows ===
In addition to acting, Lemasters is also a singer and lead guitarist for the band Wallows with Cole Preston (drums), and Dylan Minnette (singer, guitar). The band won the Battle Of The Bands Contest (2010) sponsored by radio station KYSR in 2010 and played on the 2011 Vans Warped Tour. They have since performed at several popular Los Angeles venues including the Roxy Theater and Whisky a Go Go.
The band began releasing songs independently in April 2017 starting with "Pleaser", which reached number two on the Spotify Global Viral 50 chart.
In 2018, Wallows signed a deal with Atlantic Records and released their major label debut EP, Spring, including singles "Pictures of Girls" and "These Days". They performed "Pictures of Girls" on The Late Late Show with James Corden on May 8, 2018. The band released their debut studio album, Nothing Happens, in 2019, which featured the hit single "Are You Bored Yet?" In 2020, they released their second EP Remote
